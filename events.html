Every part of this day has been intentionally curated — from the breathtaking setting to the incredible facilitators, movement, nourishing food, sound healing, sacred circles, and meaningful conversations. My hope is that every woman leaves feeling more grounded, more connected, and more like herself.

Not because we need fixing. But because every woman deserves the opportunity to come home to herself.

Whether you're navigating a new season of life, longing for deeper connection, or simply craving a day to pause, breathe, and reconnect… this gathering is for you.

As you read this, if a woman comes to mind — a sister, a friend, a colleague, or a neighbor who would be nourished by a day like this — I hope you'll invite her.

My dream is to gather women of every generation and every walk of life into one beautiful circle where we remember that healing grows in community, belonging, and shared experience. I would be deeply honored to have you there.

Liya Garber Kundalini Activation & Manifestation Leader

Liya is a Kundalini Activation & Manifestation Leader. She's also an E-RYT 500 yoga and meditation teacher known for her joyful presence, intuitive wisdom, and deep commitment to women's empowerment. Based in the Bay Area, she teaches weekly classes, leads global retreats, and is the founder of Aura Kundalini Yoga—an innovative online studio that blends Kundalini energy work with modern mind-reprogramming practices. After stepping away from a demanding executive career in New York City, Liya turned to Kundalini Yoga and holistic healing, ultimately restoring her thyroid health and reconnecting with her true path. This profound transformation fuels her passion for creating safe, uplifting spaces where others can heal, expand their consciousness, and step into their fullest potential. Her teachings are shaped by immersive experiences in spiritual hubs such as Guatemala and India, giving her approach both authenticity and a fresh, progressive edge. Beyond her classes and retreats, Liya leads the Self-Mastery Kundalini Yoga Teacher Training (220-hour), empowering students with the practices, tools, and awareness to embody their gifts and inspire meaningful change. The next training begins in March 2026 and is now open for enrollment.

Mana Shams Wisdom Keeper, Spiritual Guide & Mystic Dancer

Mana Shams is a wisdom keeper, spiritual guide, mystic dancer, and space holder devoted to helping people return home to themselves. Drawing from years of study and embodied practice, she creates experiences that gently reconnect individuals with their inner wisdom through presence, grounding, embodiment, energetic awareness, and the healing power of sound, breath, and meditation. At this soulful gathering, Mana will guide participants through grounding practices, gratitude, and immersive sound healing, offering a sanctuary where the nervous system can soften, the heart can open, and deep restoration can begin.

With a unique background that bridges science and spirituality, Mana is trained in Intuition Medicine©, bioenergetic healing, somatic practices, and bioresonance sound therapy. Her work weaves together ancient wisdom with a modern understanding of the body, along with her experience as a medical medium and mentor, supporting people in accessing their intuition and nurturing emotional, physical, and spiritual well-being. Whether navigating grief, divorce, journey of physical healing, life transitions, becoming an empty nester, seeking creativity, or deepening a connection to the sacred, Mana offers practical tools and meaningful practices that can be integrated into everyday life. Her intention is to help people cultivate greater presence, resilience, and a deeper, more compassionate relationship with themselves and the sacred life.

Tiffany Rowe Facilitator, Coach & Restorative Yogi

I am a facilitator/coach, restorative yogi, and mental health advocate doing my best every day to live with courage and vulnerability. My work is grounded in my lived experience as a human who has faced a LOT of "reality" in my time on earth and has tried the fixing, hiding, stuffing, avoiding, armoring up, and fresh-starting. The only thing that has actually helped me find my way through and forward is allowing myself to feel and heal; a choice that requires a strong and open heart, an appreciation for the necessary complexity of being human, and self-trust above all else. That's really hard sometimes and always worth it.

What's it like to spend time with me? You get the same person whether you know me inside or outside of my practice…I'm proud to be in my 50s and grateful to have made it here. I pretty much always wear jeans and fun shoes but I love to change my hair and have given up keeping my "headshot" current. I watch Inside Out at least 4 times every year, prefer popcorn to lobster, buy more books than I have time to read, cry when I'm happy and sad, cheer out loud when people win on The Price is Right, and have given back to Make-A-Wish for 38 years to pay forward the impact of my own wish. I have the unique perspective and sideways sense of humor often shared by people who have walked through fire…both of which have really come in handy while building an adult life that I love even on the days it flattens me.

Years ago I found a quote that sums it all up… "see the world as it is and know you have the grit & grace to choose a path of love". We trust ourselves, fully engage with our hearts open to life, take the joys and the hits in stride, do our best with what we have, intentionally recover, get curious, learn, and try again.

Because this is the bravest, kindest, most resilient way to live and lead.

Taylor Houston Chef & Holistic Nutrition Practitioner

Taylor Houston is a trained chef and holistic nutrition practitioner whose relationship with food began in professional kitchens — culinary school, and years working as a baker, chef, and event coordinator across restaurants, bakeries, and wineries. That fast-paced world eventually led to burnout and health struggles that became the doorway into her own healing, sending her deeper into holistic nutrition, clinical herbalism, and Ayurvedic medicine.

Today her cooking is built on simple, high-quality, whole-food ingredients — intuitive rather than technical, rooted in the belief that a shared meal is one of the most powerful acts of nourishment and connection there is. At Wildly Rooted, Taylor will bring that philosophy to life, preparing a meal for the gathering designed to nourish body and spirit alike — food as an offering of care for every woman at the table.
